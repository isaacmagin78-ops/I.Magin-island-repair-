# The Wildlife Center at Legends Ranch — Fundraising Playbook

Prepared July 2026. Every fact below is from public records or the
center's own public pages; items marked (verify) should be confirmed
with Arturo/Robert before publishing anywhere.

## The organization (verified public data)

- Legal name: **Gutierrez Wildlife Center** — EIN **81-4613373**
- 501(c)(3) public charity, IRS ruling 2018; donations tax-deductible
- Official site: wildlifecentermi.org • Facebook: facebook.com/wildlifecentermi
- Location: Bitely, MI (Newaygo County), at Legends Ranch, along the
  Manistee National Forest
- Facility: 24,000 sq ft; 3,000+ full-mount animals — the largest
  private taxidermy collection on the continent
- Founder: Arthur Gutierrez, Sr. — holds 5 of the top 10 SCI estate
  whitetail records; collection built over decades of world hunting
- Programs: free student field trips (thousands of Michigan students
  per year, ~15 curriculum stations), Learn to Hunt, Youth Challenge
  Hunt, Purple Heart Hunts for wounded veterans, event/venue rental
- **Finances (2024, per public 990 listings): revenue ~$299k,
  expenses ~$361k, assets ~$1.83M.** The center currently spends more
  than it raises — roughly a $60k/year gap. That gap is the fundraising
  target to name explicitly in every appeal.

## Why this is a strong fundraising story

1. One-of-a-kind asset: "the continent's largest private taxidermy
   collection" is a superlative no other Michigan nonprofit owns.
2. Free-for-kids model: donors love funding things children get for
   free. "Your $X keeps field trips free" is concrete and provable.
3. Veterans programming: Purple Heart Hunts attract a donor audience
   (veteran families, patriotic businesses) far beyond hunting circles.
4. Founder legacy: an 80s-something founder's life work creates urgency
   and estate-gift potential ("help his legacy outlive him").

## Twelve concrete revenue moves (ordered by effort)

### Now (no new infrastructure)
1. **Name the gap.** Add to every page/appeal: "It costs ~$1,000 a day
   to keep the Center open and field trips free." (Derive exact figure
   from the 990; verify before use.)
2. **Sponsor-a-Field-Trip.** $500 (verify per-trip cost) puts a named
   school's bus, admission, and materials on one donor. Businesses buy
   these in blocks; put the sponsor's name on that day's welcome sign.
3. **Adopt-a-Mount.** 3,000 mounts = 3,000 naming opportunities. Tiered
   ($100 songbird → $5,000 for the marquee whitetails) with a plaque
   and a certificate. Nothing to build — the inventory already exists.
4. **The film's donate CTA.** The new Wildlife Center film ends on
   "Every gift keeps the doors open — wildlifecentermi.org." Run it on
   the website, Facebook, and in the lobby on loop.
5. **Facebook fundraising tools.** The page already has engaged Reels
   (1K–5K views). Turn on FB/Instagram donate buttons (available to
   501c3s) and pin a birthday-fundraiser ask.

### Next 90 days
6. **Michigan school-district outreach kit.** One PDF: curriculum
   alignment, bus logistics, zero cost. Every district that books
   becomes a story for donors ("we hosted 14 districts last year").
7. **Legacy Society ("The Founder's Circle").** Estate gifts and
   multi-year pledges pitched around Arthur's story. Even 3–5 pledges
   changes a $300k nonprofit's trajectory.
8. **Corporate memberships.** The Center already hosts events; sell
   annual corporate tiers (venue credits + logo wall + field-trip
   sponsorships bundled).
9. **Annual gala at the Center itself.** Dinner among the mounts is an
   unrepeatable venue. Auction one Legends Ranch hunt (donated by the
   for-profit ranch) as the headline lot — one lot can clear $20k+.

### Grants (institutional money)
10. **Conservation-education grants.** Target: Michigan-based family
    foundations, community foundations (Fremont Area Community
    Foundation is the Newaygo County anchor — verify fit), NRA
    Foundation, Safari Club International Foundation, MUCC partners,
    and outdoor-industry giving programs (Bass Pro/Cabela's Outdoor
    Fund, Sportsman's Warehouse Foundation).
11. **Veteran-services grants** for the Purple Heart Hunts: Disabled
    Veterans National Foundation, Bob Woodruff Foundation, local VFW/
    American Legion posts, Home Depot Foundation (accessibility).
12. **State/federal:** Michigan DNR outdoor-education and R3
    (recruitment/retention/reactivation) grant programs; IMLS museum
    grants (the collection plausibly qualifies as a museum).

## Rules to keep it clean

- The 501(c)(3) (Wildlife Center) and the for-profit ranch (Legends)
  must stay clearly separated in money and messaging. Donations fund
  education/veterans/collection care — never hunts sold by the ranch.
- Put the EIN (81-4613373) and "donations are tax-deductible" on every
  donation page and receipt.
- File and publicize Form 990 on the website (donors and grantmakers
  check); a Charity Navigator/Candid (GuideStar) profile claim is free
  and unlocks many corporate-matching programs.
